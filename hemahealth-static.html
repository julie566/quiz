Understanding Sickle Cell Disease

Learn how sickle cell disease affects red blood cells and the body.

Healthy Cell

🩸 Healthy Red Blood Cell

A healthy red blood cell is shaped like a round, flat disc — flexible enough to squeeze through even the tiniest blood vessels. Inside, it carries a protein called haemoglobin, which picks up oxygen in the lungs and delivers it to every part of the body. Each cell lives for about 120 days before being naturally replaced by the bone marrow.

Did you know?

Your body produces around 2 million new red blood cells every second!

Sickle Cell

⚠️ Sickle Cell

A sickle cell is shaped like a crescent or sickle — rigid and unable to bend. This shape is caused by a change in a single gene inherited from both parents, which makes haemoglobin clump together. Because sickle cells are stiff, they get stuck in blood vessels and block oxygen flow, causing pain crises. They also die much faster — only 10–20 days — leading to chronic anaemia.

Did you know?

Just one tiny DNA change — out of 3 billion — is enough to cause this difference in cell shape.

Key Facts

🧬

Genetic Condition

A child must receive the mutated gene from both parents to develop sickle cell disease.

😣

Pain Crises

Sickle cells block blood vessels causing sudden severe pain episodes lasting hours or days.

🩺

Anaemia

Because sickle cells die so quickly, the body struggles to maintain enough red blood cells.

💊

Management

Treatments including medication and bone marrow transplants can help manage symptoms.
