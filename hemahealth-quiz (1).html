Red Blood Cells:

Healthy Cell

🩸 Healthy Red Blood Cell

A healthy red blood cell is shaped like a round, flat disc — flexible enough to squeeze through even the tiniest blood vessels. Inside, it carries a protein called haemoglobin, which picks up oxygen in the lungs and delivers it to every part of the body. Each cell lives for about 120 days before being naturally replaced.

Did you know?

Your body produces around 2 million new red blood cells every second!

Sickle Cell

⚠️ Sickle Cell

A sickle cell is shaped like a crescent — rigid and unable to bend. This is caused by a change in a single gene inherited from both parents, which makes haemoglobin clump together. Sickle cells get stuck in blood vessels and block oxygen flow, causing pain crises. They also die in just 10–20 days, leading to chronic anaemia.

Did you know?

Just one tiny DNA change — out of 3 billion — causes this difference in cell shape.
